### Introduction

From the beginning, Intentional Society has been rooted in a desire to become our 'biggest selves' — with the capabilities and capacity to navigate the ever-increasing complexity of the world we inhabit.

Whether that world is the rich interior landscape of our inner life, the relationships we hold dear with our friends and family, or our broader relationship to community and society as a whole, we strive to grow.

Together, we've embarked on a journey of connection fuelled by intention. It's a journey that asks us to become more aware, more spacious, and more flexible in how we show up. It asks us to examine, shift and redefine our relationships with ourselves, with others, and with the world we live in.

We do this by coming together regularly to practice. To practice being together in a way that shifts our understanding of ourselves and each other. To practice entering into friendship with awareness, acceptance, and integrity. Together, we strive to be better at being the humans we want to be.

Relational practices are how we help ourselves grow. Here you will find some of our favourites.

Each practice stands alone and can, in itself, contribute to increasing our capacity for awareness, acceptance, and integrity. We have ordered these practices roughly in the direction of increasing scope of relationality — beginning with our relationship with ourselves (the 'I'), moving to interpersonal relating (the 'We'), then turning our focus outward to the larger environment (the 'World').

Although the practices have been arranged this way, they are a kind of dance. We move back and forth across them, without claiming mastery before moving on. From the inner to the outer and back again, there is a complementary dance between the scopes as we discover who we are becoming by relating.

Almost all of these practices are not of our own creation. Many groups and friends also believe that connection and relationship are at the heart of building a better world, and we respectfully acknowledge their work in helping us grow ourselves.

### How to Use This Catalog

Think of this catalog like a recipe book. Each entry includes:

- An introduction to the practice and its origins
- What the practice is and why it matters
- How to run it — the 'recipe'
- Friends and references for deeper exploration

The practices are grouped into three sections that mirror the three territories of growth at Intentional Society:

- Inner-Shaded — practices oriented toward the 'I': awareness of self, our inner landscape, and the gap between our intentions and their impact
- Interpersonal — practices oriented toward the 'We': deepening our capacity to be with and understand one another
- Outer-Shaded — practices oriented toward the 'World': engaging with collective wisdom, environmental challenges, and our relationship to larger systems
